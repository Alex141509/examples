Syllabus

Welcome to LIT 339: Shakespeare in Film. This course explores the interpretation of Shakespeare's works in various film adaptations.

  • Hamlet (Branagh, 1996)
  • Romeo + Juliet (Luhrmann, 1996)
  • Othello (Parker, 1995)

Watching Requirements

Films, when not available on major streaming platforms, will be accessible via the university's media library.

Objectives

  • Students will analyze the adaptation of Shakespeare's works in cinema.
  • Students will write analytical essays on Shakespeare's plays and their film adaptations.
  • Students will understand the relationship between Shakespeare's texts and cinematic techniques.

Assignments

  • Quizzes: Unannounced quizzes on reading material.
  • Midterm Paper: A 5-page analysis of a film.
  • Final Paper: A 10-page research paper on a film adaptation not covered in class.
  • Play Review: Attend and review The Tempest at Chicago Shakespeare Theater.
  • Final Exam: Short answer and essay questions covering course material.

Grading

  • Quizzes: 15%
  • Midterm Paper: 20%
  • Final Paper: 30%
  • Play Review: 15%
  • Final Exam: 20%

Course Policies

Attendance

Attendance is mandatory. Missing more than 4 classes will lower your grade by one letter. More than 6 absences will result in automatic failure.

Late Assignments

Late assignments will lose 10% per day. Assignments more than 3 days late will not be accepted.

Plagiarism

Plagiarism will result in a failing grade for the assignment and may result in failure of the course.

Class Participation

Active participation is required and will factor into your final grade.
